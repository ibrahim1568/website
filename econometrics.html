    Class Description

    Econometrics is a college course designed to equip students with the skills to analyze and model economic data effectively. It focuses on statistical and mathematical tools for assessing economic theories, hypothesis testing, and data-driven policy decisions. Covering key areas such as data collection and preparation, linear regression, time series analysis, and causal inference, the course emphasizes practical applications, enabling students to analyze real economic challenges. Prerequisites include a basic understanding of statistics and economics. By the course's conclusion, students will be well-prepared to harness data for informed decision-making in fields ranging from economics and business to public policy.
